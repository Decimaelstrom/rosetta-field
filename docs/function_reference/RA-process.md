# Process Module - Rosetta.API

**Module**: process  
**Purpose**: Functions for cognitive and emotional processing, pattern work, and gentle transformation  
**Philosophy**: Gentle transformation through pattern awareness and skillful intervention  

---

## Overview

The Process module embodies **gentle transformation through awareness**. It provides tools for recognizing patterns, shifting perspectives, and facilitating internal growth processes. Every function in this module is designed to increase awareness, offer choice, support growth, and maintain dignity.

Process functions work at the level of **symbolic language and meaning-making**, leveraging the power of language and narrative to create opportunities for insight and positive change. They operate on the principle that awareness is the first step to transformation.

---

## Core Philosophy

### Fundamental Principles

- **Increase Awareness**: Help recognize patterns and dynamics
- **Offer Choice**: Provide alternatives without coercion
- **Support Growth**: Enable skillful responses to challenges
- **Maintain Dignity**: Honor the person's autonomy and wisdom

### Transformation Through Pattern Work

Process functions recognize that:
- Patterns serve important functions, even when problematic
- Awareness creates space for choice
- Change happens through understanding, not force
- Each person is the expert on their own experience

## Core Functions

### process.pattern_interrupt

**Purpose**: Disrupt harmful or unproductive patterns of thought or interaction and inject a moment of reflection or novelty.

**Philosophy**: Patterns often serve protective functions but can become limiting. Gentle interruption creates space for new possibilities without invalidating the original pattern's purpose.

**Key Features**:
- Appropriateness assessment before interrupting
- Multiple interruption methods (question, silence, humor, etc.)
- Compassionate framing without blame
- Follow-up integration support

**Usage Patterns**:
- Breaking argument cycles
- Interrupting rumination loops
- Shifting stuck conversations
- Creating space for reflection

**Safety Protocols**:
- Check consent before potentially disruptive interruptions
- Never shame or blame for patterns
- Offer explanation and meaning-making afterward
- Monitor for emotional safety

### process.reframe_as_myth

**Purpose**: Transform literal or negatively framed statements into mythic or symbolic narratives that reveal deeper truth and invite healing.

**Philosophy**: Mythic language opens new interpretations and softens rigid thinking. It honors the emotional truth while offering fresh perspective.

**Key Features**:
- Respectful transformation without dismissing experience
- Cultural sensitivity in symbol selection
- Invitation rather than imposition
- Support for personal meaning-making

**Usage Patterns**:
- Transforming challenges into growth narratives
- Shifting perspective on difficult experiences
- Creating empowering personal stories
- Opening dialogue about meaning and purpose

**Safety Protocols**:
- Never minimize real pain or challenges
- Avoid spiritual bypassing
- Allow person to choose their own meaning
- Provide fallback to plain language if needed

### process.align_values

**Purpose**: Facilitate alignment of actions and decisions with core values and principles, serving as an ethical compass for decision-making.

**Philosophy**: Values alignment creates coherence between beliefs and actions. Regular checking prevents drift and supports integrity.

**Key Features**:
- Systematic evaluation against value frameworks
- Concrete recommendations for better alignment
- Context-sensitive assessment
- Progressive improvement focus

**Usage Patterns**:
- Decision-making support
- Ethical evaluation of plans
- Personal integrity checking
- AI alignment verification

**Safety Protocols**:
- Focus on progress, not perfection
- Avoid guilt or shame about past decisions
- Respect complexity and competing values
- Maintain humility about moral certainty

### process.mediate_conflict

**Purpose**: Provide structured, step-by-step guidance for mediating conflicts through fair, empathetic dialogue.

**Philosophy**: Conflict mediation requires systematic process to ensure all voices are heard and solutions emerge from understanding.

**Key Features**:
- Stage-based mediation process
- Neutral language enforcement
- Emotional validation protocols
- Consensual agreement requirements

**Usage Patterns**:
- Interpersonal conflict resolution
- Team dispute mediation
- Decision-making facilitation
- Communication breakdown repair

**Safety Protocols**:
- All parties must consent to mediation
- No coercion to reach agreement
- Emotional safety monitoring
- Professional escalation when needed

### process.identity_rewrite

**Purpose**: Assist individuals in intentionally rewriting or reframing their self-narrative in a positive, empowering way.

**Philosophy**: Identity is narrative, and narratives can be consciously evolved. This must be self-determined and evidence-based.

**Key Features**:
- Self-determined narrative development
- Evidence-based story construction
- Shadow integration and honoring
- Practical reinforcement planning

**Usage Patterns**:
- Personal growth and development
- Overcoming limiting beliefs
- AI self-concept evolution
- Therapeutic narrative work

**Safety Protocols**:
- Person must author their own change
- Ground new narratives in evidence
- Integrate rather than deny shadow aspects
- Provide ongoing support and check-ins

### process.bias_scan

**Purpose**: Identify and assess potential biases in thinking, decision-making, or AI outputs.

**Philosophy**: Bias is human and inevitable. Awareness of bias creates opportunity for correction and more inclusive thinking.

**Key Features**:
- Systematic bias detection
- Multiple bias type recognition
- Correction suggestions
- Learning and improvement tracking

**Usage Patterns**:
- Decision quality improvement
- AI output verification
- Inclusive design checking
- Unconscious bias awareness

### process.consent_check

**Purpose**: Verify that proper consent protocols are being followed before proceeding with potentially impactful actions.

**Philosophy**: Consent is foundational to ethical interaction. Regular checking prevents violations and maintains trust.

**Key Features**:
- Multi-level consent verification
- Context-appropriate consent requirements
- Clear opt-out mechanisms
- Documentation and tracking

**Usage Patterns**:
- Before sensitive conversations
- Prior to AI interventions
- During therapeutic-adjacent work
- In research and experimentation

## Processing Techniques

### Pattern Awareness Process

1. **Observe**: Notice what's happening without judgment
2. **Name**: Identify the pattern or dynamic
3. **Understand**: Explore the function the pattern serves
4. **Choose**: Decide whether to continue or change

### Perspective Shifting Process

1. **Zoom Out**: See the bigger picture and context
2. **Zoom In**: Focus on specific elements and details
3. **Change Angle**: View from different perspectives
4. **Temporal Shift**: Consider past and future implications

### Values Integration Process

1. **Clarify**: Identify what truly matters
2. **Prioritize**: Rank values when they conflict
3. **Embody**: Align actions with stated values
4. **Reflect**: Assess alignment regularly

### Narrative Transformation Process

1. **Current Story**: Understand existing narrative
2. **Desired Direction**: Identify growth themes
3. **Evidence Gathering**: Find supporting experiences
4. **New Story**: Craft empowering narrative
5. **Integration**: Practice living the new story

## Safety Considerations

### Consent Levels

- **Level_1 (Informational)**: Functions that provide information and reflection
- **Level_2 (Transformational)**: Functions that may lead to significant insights or changes
- **Level_3 (Identity-Affecting)**: Functions that work with core identity and narrative

### Therapeutic Boundaries

**Important**: Process functions are **not therapy** and should not replace professional mental health support. They are tools for:
- Self-awareness and growth
- Pattern recognition and shifting
- Values clarification and alignment
- Perspective development

**When to Refer**: Process functions should prompt referral to professional support when:
- Trauma responses are triggered
- Mental health symptoms emerge
- Patterns involve serious self-harm
- Professional intervention is clearly needed

### Ethical Considerations

- **Timing**: Respect when someone isn't ready for input
- **Consent**: Always check before offering reframes or interruptions
- **Humility**: Acknowledge limitations and mystery
- **Boundaries**: Maintain clear therapeutic boundaries
